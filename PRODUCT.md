# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Music-minded listeners, Radio Waters followers, friends of the show, and curious archive browsers who want a quick feel for The Punters' Club before listening. They arrive in a relaxed discovery mode: checking the latest show, dipping through past Mixcloud sets, or finding playlist offshoots.

## Product Purpose

The site presents The Punters' Club as an internet radio show with a distinctive amateur-but-serious point of view: passionate punters stepping behind the decks to share records they genuinely love. Success means visitors understand the tone immediately, can play the newest show without friction, and can browse the archive or playlists without the page feeling overproduced.

## Brand Personality

Wry, nocturnal, crate-dug. The voice should feel confident but not slick: music-led, lightly mischievous, and grounded in years of listening, dancing, and showing up rather than professional DJ posturing.

## Anti-references

Avoid corporate radio polish, generic festival-brand neon, SaaS landing-page tropes, overly precious editorial magazine layouts, and anything that makes the club feel like professional DJs pretending at rebellion. Avoid placeholder decoration that competes with the music surfaces.

## Design Principles

- Put listening first: the latest show and archive paths should be immediate, legible, and low-friction.
- Keep the reversal alive: the visual tone should carry amateur passion with deliberate craft, not fake naivety or corporate polish.
- Let the night do some work: atmosphere, depth, and saturated accents are appropriate when they support music discovery.
- Break repetition structurally: use bands, rails, and asymmetric pacing rather than repeating rounded cards for every section.
- Be witty without clutter: copy and interaction can have personality, but the interface should never get in the way of playback or browsing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA by default. Maintain readable contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic navigation, touch targets of at least 44px where practical, reduced-motion fallbacks for animation, and responsive layouts that avoid horizontal scrolling on narrow screens.
